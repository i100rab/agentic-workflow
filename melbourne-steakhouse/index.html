The Short Version

Three things we don’t compromise on

Dry-aged in-house

35 to 55 days in our own ageing room. Gippsland and Rangers Valley beef only, graded by hand each week.

Ironbark & redgum coals

Every steak finishes over native hardwood embers at close to 600°C. No gas grill in the building.

A short, honest menu

Twelve dishes, changed by season rather than by trend. If it’s on the menu, we’re proud of it.

How a Steak Gets Here

Four stages, no shortcuts

  1. Select

    Whole primals sourced weekly from Gippsland farms and the Rangers Valley program, graded by hand.

  2. Age

    35 to 55 days in a humidity-controlled room until the flavour concentrates and the texture changes.

  3. Sear

    Finished over ironbark and redgum coals, close to 600°C, for a crust you can hear from the pass.

  4. Rest

    Rested tableside before the first cut, so the juice stays in the meat and not on the board.
