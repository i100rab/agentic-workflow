Our Fire

Cooked the way it should be, not the way it’s easiest.

The Room

Eighteen tables down a Melbourne CBD laneway, in a room that used to be a print workshop. Exposed brick, low light, and a pass you can see from most seats — because half the point of a steakhouse is watching the fire work.

The Beef

We buy whole primals from two sources: Gippsland grass-fed farms for everyday cuts, and the Rangers Valley program for anything that needs longer marbling. Everything is dry-aged on site, in a room held at 1–2°C and 82% humidity, checked by hand every Monday.

The Kitchen

Head chef Marcus Ilic spent twelve years cooking over fire, including four running the grill section at a well-known Sydney steakhouse, before opening Ironbark’s kitchen. He still does the dry-age checks himself, every Monday morning, and the grill section reports directly to him on service nights.

We don’t chase every cuisine. We do one thing — buying, ageing, and firing beef — and we try to do it better each month than the one before. — Marcus Ilic, Head Chef
